【课程简介】
In the contemporary era, state and state institutions, corporate organizations, and pressure groups are competing for space in the media in their pursuit of legitimacy, organizational interest, or attention. Understanding the complex process through which this occurs requires knowledge of the techniques of political communication. The terms “propaganda”, “public relations” (PR), “persuasion” and “public diplomacy” can also be used to describe this phenomena. The study of the relationship between the media and the various forms of guided communications takes us closer to understanding of some of the key concerns and debates about changing media relations to wider structures and systems of power in society.
 
In this course students will be looking at the history and nature of government- directed intercultural communication, propaganda, and other aspects of political communication by close examination of case studies in different historical moments as well as the contemporary era. A key aim of the course is to equip students with critical skills to enable them to analyse media texts in relation to propaganda, public relations, and other means to mould public opinion, and to further reflect on theories of media and power in society.
 
【课程目标】
1、Develop an understanding of key terms and concepts such as propaganda, public diplomacy, political communication, transcultural communication, Public Relations, Spin, persuasion, legitimacy, ideology, hegemony etc.
2、Develop skills and competence in analysing media texts in relation to transcultural communication, political communication, persuasion, propaganda and PR.
3、Develop an understanding of current debates on transcultural communication, political communication, propaganda and PR in contemporary society.
4、Understand how changing media technologies as well as changes in structure of society are raising further questions about existing models of media and power in society.
5、Have improved their ability to communicate using written and oral English in an academic environment.
 
【教师简介】
新西兰坎特伯雷大学语言、社会与政治科学学院，政治与国际关系学系Anne Marie Brady（安琳）教授。安琳教授的研究方向是中国的国内政治和外交政策以及两极政治。她的普通话流利，用中文完成了政治学和国际关系的双专位。
 
安琳教授在奥克兰大学就师从John Minford，并在澳大利亚国立大学跟随博士生导师Geremie R. Barmé和W.J.F Jenner完成了博士学位。她的研究兴趣十分广泛，涉及冷战、能源、南北极、亚洲、中国大陆、日本、朝鲜、南亚、韩国、东南亚和台湾。
 
安琳教授近期的著作包括：《让外国服务中国：管理好在中国的外国人》,《市场专制：当代中国的宣传和思想工作》，《向北看，往南看——中国，台湾和南太平洋》，《中国的思想管理》，《南极洲的新兴政治》和《中国，世界力量的一极》。
 
Anne-Marie researches Chinese domestic and foreign politics and polar politics. She is editor-in-chief of The Polar Journal, and has published ten books and more than forty scholarly papers on a range of issues including China’s strategic interests in the Arctic and Antarctic, China’s modernised propaganda system, New Zealand-China relations, NZ foreign policy and competing foreign policy interests in Antarctica. She is a fluent Mandarin speaker with dual majors in Chinese, Political Science and International Relations. At Auckland University she studied with John Minford and at Australian National University her doctoral supervisors were Geremie R. Barmé and W.J.F Jenner. All three instilled in her an acute ear for the political use of language and a deep respect for primary sources.
 
Anne-Marie is a Global Fellow at the Woodrow Wilson Centre in Washington DC, a non-resident Senior Fellow at the China Policy Institute at the University of Nottingham, and a member of the Council on Security Cooperation in the Asia-Pacific. In 2014 she was appointed to a two-year term on the World Economic Forum's Global Action Council on the Arctic.
 
Her recent publications include: Making the Foreign Serve China: Managing Foreigners in the People's Republic (Rowman and Littlefield, 2003), Marketing Dictatorship: Propaganda and Thought Work in Contemporary China (Rowman and Littlefield, 2008), Looking North, Looking South: China, Taiwan, and the South Pacific (World Scientific, 2010), China's Thought Management (Routledge, 2012), The Emerging Politics of Antarctica (Routledge, 2013) and China as a Polar Great Power (Cambridge University Press and Wilson Press, 2017).
