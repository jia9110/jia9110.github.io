美国研究型大学的教与学――康州大学经验分享

Orchestrating culture change at a research intensive university: How we made the case that teaching and learning really matter

时间： 10 月 16 日中午 12:30

地点：苏世民书院 B1 层 华夏幸福报告厅

语言：英文

报告人简介：

Peter Diplock, PhD

Peter Diplock is the Assistant Vice-Provost for the University of Connecticut’s Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning (CETL), a role he has occupied since 2012. Peter provides thought leadership for teaching initiatives at the University and with a talented staff of 50 has responsibility for elevating teaching and learning outcomes across all teaching modalities including flipped, blended, and online environments. CETL’s Faculty Development and Teaching Enhancement initiatives include support for teaching excellence and innovation through workshops, course-design and teaching consultations, annual grant programs, dissemination of research-informed best practices, programs that work to align pedagogy with technology, and support for various faculty communities of practice.   Last year, more than 1,100 UConn faculty and TA’s took advantage of CETL programming.   Peter joined UConn in 2005 as a faculty member in Management and has been the recipient of numerous teaching awards over his twenty year teaching career including the Distinguished Teaching Award from the University of Massachusetts.

 

 

Stuart Paul Duncan, PhD

Stuart Paul Duncan, originally from England, is the Director for New Faculty and Teaching Assistant Development for the Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning at the University of Connecticut (UConn).   He completed a doctorate in music composition from Cornell University (2010) and a PhD in music theory from Yale University (2017), publishing multiple papers on the topic of rhythm and meter in the music of Benjamin Britten.   While completing his PhD at Yale, Stuart also worked with Yale’s Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning and was a faculty member in the music department at UConn where he received the University’s distinguished teaching award.   Stuart has coauthored a book chapter in The Oxford Handbook of Social Justice in Music Education entitled "Behind Different Walls: Restorative and Transformative Justice and their relationship to Music Education” and is finishing a book on the topic of music education in prisons. Currently, Stuart is pursuing research in the area of gamification.
