【教师简历】

Sintomer教授是国际知名的政治社会学家，致力于审议民主（协商民主）、政治代表制的研究。他曾协调主持了“比较视野中的欧洲参与式预算”等跨国调查研究项目。Sintomer教授现在是巴黎八大政治学系的政治社会学教授，马克﹒布劳克研究中心（柏林）副主任，同时也是法国国家科研中心与巴黎八大联合成立的城市社会学-政治社会学研究中心、瑞士纳沙泰尔大学社会学研究所、洛桑大学政治与国际研究中心成员，哈佛大学访问教授。Sintomer教授著述颇丰，最新的一本著作是2011年11月出版的研究抽签民主的《民主实验史：从雅典到今天的民主命运与民主政治》(Petite histoire de l'expérimentation démocratique：Tirage ausort et politique d'Athènes à nos jours)。由于其卓越的研究，2012年他被选为法国大学研究院资深院士(senior fellow of Institut Universitaire de France)。

 

【课程简介】

前苏东社会主义国家转型之后，福山在《历史的终结及最后的人》中写到，自由民主是“人类意识形态的终点”，也是“人类政治制度的最终形式”，因此构成了“历史的终结”，自由民主不可能有进一步的改进形式了。但不到30年后，特朗普的当选、英国脱欧以及右翼排外民粹主义的兴起，以及新的全球成员尤其是中国的崛起等一系列现象都表明《历史的终结》只是一种偏激的观点，看起来已经过时了。尽管很多威权体制下生活的人们向往民主，但是西方政治模式的合法性危机正在加深并扩散到全球范围。 在此背景下，本课程将围绕两个问题展开：（1）解释西方政治制度怎么样以及为什么会兴起、达到顶峰而后开始衰败；（2）将这种模式区域化，把它放置在一个特殊政治地理环境和一个特定历史阶段进行理解，既有部分普世化的特征，也有部分例外之处。

课程旨在帮助学生批判性地理解西方政治制度。课程将以英文讲授，鼓励学生参与讨论交流，锻炼用英语进行学术交流表达的技巧与能力。课程评价包括两个部分：（1）出席与参与；（2）完成一篇课程论文。

 

【课程大纲】

The course will be organized around seven topics, one for each session.

1.Is there a “Western” political system? Connected histories and the invention of a tradition (15.20-16.55 Dec 7th -2 TH). The great narrative of the Western political system usually starts with the Greeks, follows with the Christian middle age and with the Renaissance, and culminates with the modern nation states and democracies. This invented tradition underestimates the historical discontinuities which took place and the imports the West made from other cultures (including China). Although germs, guns, commodities and pollution travel much more easily than political ideas, models and institutions, political transfers and hybridizations are much more frequent than usually thought. Learning from the lessons of Global histories and connected histories, this session will present an alternative picture and will explain the objective of the course.

 

Text: Armitage David, Subrahmanyam Sanjay, The Age of Revolutions in Global Context, c. 1760-1840 , Basingstoke: Palgrave Macmillan, 2010 (“Introduction”).

 

2.The rise of the Western model (19.20-21.50 Dec 9th -3 TH). The second session will present the genealogy of the pillars of the modern Western political system: the nation state and sovereignty, representative government, the rule of law, the free public sphere. It will underline the Christian roots of these pillars, but also the dimensions that were imported and hybridized. One example is the representative government and the exams to become civil servants, that both represent a combination of selecting the best few and recognizing the equality of the many, with roots within the Christian but also within the Chinese tradition. The session will stress the continuities and discontinuities between the Ancient Regimes and the American and French revolutions. We will try to differentiate the most universalizable and the more local features of the system.

 

Text: Balibar Etienne, Equaliberty: Political Essays , Durham (North Carolina): Duke University Press, 2014 (“Chapter 1: The Proposition of Equaliberty”).

 

3.The two faces of the Liberty of the (Western) Moderns (19.20-21.50 Dec 10th -3TH). The third session will analyze the two faces of the Western political model: a face of liberty and empowerment for the insiders, and of exclusion and domination for the outsiders. Both faces are equally true: liberty and empowerment for insiders cannot be denied, by nor the exclusion and domination for outsiders. Both sides, and their connections, The Western model is therefore be understood in a context of competing empires, and as a dialectic of freedom and domination.

 

Text: Rana Aziz, The Two Faces of American Freedom , Cambridge (Mass.)/London: Harvard University Press, 2014.

 

4.Imports, hybridizations, innovations: the reception of the Western model in India and China (19.20-20.55 Dec 11th -2 TH) . The mainstream narrative in the West describes the influence of its political system in the following way: inventions in the Global north, and then more or less successful diffusion of the model in the Global South. The course will defend another narrative: imports have generally hybridized the Western models and a lot of innovations have taken place, some of them being later imported in the Global North. Three examples will be given: the discussion about representative government in China and Japan at the end of the 19th and at the beginning of the 20th century; the discussion about quotas and “reserve seats” for lower casts in the legislative in India after World war two; and the international transfers of deliberative, consultative and participatory democracy between Latin America, the Global North and China at the turn of the 20th and 21st centuries.

 

Texts: Bajpai Rochana, Debating Difference. Group Rights and Liberal Democracy in India , New Delhi: Oxford University Press, 2016 (“Introduction”);

 

Sintomer Yves, Herzberg Carsten, Röcke Anja, “Transnational Models of Citizen Participation: The Case of Participatory Budgeting”, Journal of Public Deliberation , vol. 8, 2, 30/12/2012

 

5.Rise, peak and decay of the welfare state and Keynesian capitalism, and of liberal democracy (15.20-16.55 Dec 14th -2 TH) . Initially, one crucial aspect of the dark side of the Western model was the situation of the working class. This session will analyze the importance of the worker movement in the evolution of the West. The attractiveness of the Western political system during one or two century could not be understood without the economic and military successes of the West, but was also largely due to the rise of the welfare state and of a “Keynesian” capitalism in which the state plays an active role and in which employees and workers are protected and get a limited but real amount of welfare and social security. The course will analyze the factors, both internal (most notably mass labor-based political parties) and external (among other, the disproportionate use by the West of the raw materials produced in the all world), which explain the rise of this model and its collapse in front of neoliberalism and financial capitalism in the 1970 and 1980. We will enlighten the peak of liberal democracies after the fall of Soviet communism and their rapid decay.

 

Text : Piketty Thomas, Capital in the 21th century , Cambridge (Mass.): Harvard University Press, 2014 (“Introduction”).

 

6.News challenges: Ecological crisis and global governance (19.20-20.55 Dec 18th -2 TH). This session will explain two of the new most important challenges that the Western political model has to face in the 21th century: the ecological crisis and the global governance. The representative government cannot represent the future generations and elections tend to focus on the short term. In addition, elections have never been used and will probably never be used at transnational and even more at world level. What are the proposed political innovations that are discussed in the West in order to face the ecological crisis? Does global governance represent an alternative to the classical Western political model? The global warming will be analyzed as a litmus test.

 

Text: Aykut Stefan C., Dahan Amy, “Climate Change Governance: Anatomy of a Schism of Reality and the place of numerical technologies”, ms. 2014.

 

7.The present state and the future of politics in Western Europe and North America (15.20-16.55 Dec 21th -2 TH) . The last course will attempt to give a broad panorama of the present state of politics in Western Europe and North America, in a period which has been marked by the election of Donald Trump in the USA, the Brexit and the rise of the right-wing xenophobic populisms. We will analyze the challenges these Western regimes face when confronted to the rise of new global players, first of all China. In the conclusive part of the session, we will try to make scenario for the future of politics in Western Europe and North America in the 21st century.

 

Text: Hui Wang, “The Crisis of Representativeness and Post-Party Politics”, Modern China , 2014, vol. 40 (2), p. 214-239.
