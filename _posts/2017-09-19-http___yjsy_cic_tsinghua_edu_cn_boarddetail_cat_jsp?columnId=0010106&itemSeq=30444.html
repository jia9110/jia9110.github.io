Prof. Massa serves as Associate Editor for several academic journals including the “IEEE Transaction on Antennas and Propagation” and “International Journal of Microwave and Wireless Technologies”.
His research activities are mainly concerned with inverse problems, analysis/synthesis of antenna systems and large arrays, radar systems synthesis and signal processing, cross‐layer optimization and planning of wireless/RF systems, semantic wireless technologies, system‐by‐design and material‐by‐design, and theory/applications of optimization techniques to engineering problems. Prof. Massa published more than 600 scientific publications and organized more than 50 scientific sessions in international conferences.

He is also an Associate Researcher of IREA, an Institute on the Italian Consiglio Nazionale delle Ricerche, as well as of INFN (Istituto Nazionale di Fisica Nucleare). The scientific activities of Tommaso Isernia have been mainly concerned with the solution of non-linear inverse problems in applied electromagnetics.
The activities of Tommaso Isernia have considered the theoretical, algorithmic and experimental aspects of the problems dealt with, with particular reference to the problem of the possible occurrence of false solutions when solving the considered non linear problems.
The interest of the scientific activities of Tommaso Isernia, who is a Senior Member of IEEE, is witnessed by over one hundred publications on scientific peer reviewed (and well reputed) journals, and by an h-index equal to 30 (Scopus, February 2017). Full Professor of Electromagnetics since 2003, he has been the Dean of the studies in Ingegneria dell'Informazione at Università Mediterranea di Reggio Calabria, where he also served as a member of 'Consiglio di Amministrazione' of the whole University. 【课程简介】：
Compressive sensing (CS) is a fundamentally interdisciplinary topic, with interplay between applied/pure mathematics and engineering serving to fertilize new researches opening new frontiers. The impact of CS goes far beyond compression and classical signal processing. Whenever acquiring/inverting data/information is difficult, dangerous, or expensive, CS allows to proceed with much less data/information than previously thought possible. Such a possibility has been rapidly exploited in several and different ranges of practical electromagnetic problems almost always leading to striking results that significantly advance the state‐of‐the‐art.
 
压缩感知是一个重要的跨学科研究领域，它融合了纯数学，应用数学，和工程等多个研究领域，并开辟了新的研究前沿。压缩感知的影响已经远远超出了压缩和经典信号处理。压缩感知使我们能够利用比以前更少的数据和信息，这对于获取信息困难、危险或者昂贵的情况非常有用。压缩感知已经被迅速应用在多个实际电磁问题中，带来了良好的结果，显著地推进了最先进的技术发展。
 
本课程将邀请Andrea MASSA, Tommaso ISERNIA, Marco Donald MIGLIORE, 和Giacomo OLIVERI四位本领域的著名教授，为同学讲授压缩感知理论的基础知识，和压缩感知理论在电磁学方面的应用。四位教授将结合自己的研究专长，对稀疏性和非相关的理论及其在压缩感知方面的应用做一个系统的介绍，同时讲授压缩感知理论在天线设计，逆散射，电磁分析等方面的应用。本课程还将重点讨论压缩感知技术在实施和应用中遇到的实际挑战，课程中还会通过具体实例帮助同学理解压缩感知理论的具体实现方法，及其优势和局限性。
 
【课程大纲】：
1.      Compressive sensing (CS): introduction and theoretical basis
1.1   CS in a nutshell: the idea, and how/why can it work? (Prof. A. Massa)
1.2   Compressive processing, sparseness, and incoherence (Prof. A. Massa)
1.3   Convex formulations for CS problems: theory and examples (Prof. T. Isernia)
1.4  SW exercise : CS basics and applications to small linear problems (Prof. G. Oliveri)
 
2.       Applications of CS in antenna design and engineering
2.1   CS in antenna pattern correction: theory and examples (Prof. M. D. Migliore)
2.2   Synthesis of unconventional antenna architectures through CS (Prof. G. Oliveri)
2.3   CS for antenna analysis and design of dielectric devices (Prof. T. Isernia)
2.4  SW exercise : array design and pattern correction through CS (Prof. M. D. Migliore, prof. G. Oliveri)
 
3.       Applications of CS in inverse problems and imaging
3.1   Inverse source problems and antenna characterization by CS (Prof. M. D. Migliore)
3.2   CS imaging in standard and transformed domains (Prof. A. Massa)
3.3  CS inversion for dbim and shape reconstruction formulations (Prof. T. Isernia)
 
4.      Further issues & advanced topics in CS
4.1   Advances in CS: alternative formulations and envisaged applications (Prof. G. Oliveri, Prof. M. D. Migliore)
4.2   Concluding remarks and future trends (Prof. A. Massa, Prof. T. Isernia)
