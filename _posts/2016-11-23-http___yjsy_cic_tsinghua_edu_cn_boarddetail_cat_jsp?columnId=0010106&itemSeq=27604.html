化学系海外学者短期讲学课程：活性聚合与可控自组装
【课程名称】活性聚合与可控自组装（Living polymerization and controlled self-assembly）
【课程编号】Y0440061
【授课对象】研究生及高年级本科生
【授课人】Xiaosong Wang教授
【授课语言】英语
【上课时间】2016年11月30日，12月1日，12月14日，12月16日，晚上6:30-9:45
【上课地点】何添楼210室
【考核方式】考查，1学分
【报名方式】手动选课，即日起接受报名。请准备选课的同学在2016年11月29日之前将“学号”、“姓名” 、“所在院系”、“联系方式（邮箱地址和手机号）”等信息发送至yuanjy@mail.tsinghua.edu.cn 报名，邮件请注明“选课申请”。
【课程简介】
这门海外学者短期讲学课程讲授活性/可控自组装，属于高分子化学前沿领域，对高分子化学与物理学科的研究具有重要意义，且具有相应的深度和广度。该课程主体内容为以氢键驱动的活性超分子组装、结晶驱动的活性超分子组装、种子超分子聚合，等。
本课程适于化学系、化学工程系、材料学院、医学院、药学院等院系相关专业研究生及高年级本科生。
【教学大纲】
Course content
1. Introduction to living polymerization (4-6 lectures)
  Anionic polymerization
      --Living polymerization concept
      --Living polymerization features
      --The advantage of living polymerization
  Living condensation polymerization
      --condensation polymerization vs chain growth polymerization
      --technique for living condensation polymerization
      --conversion from step growth to chain growth
2. Living supramolecular polymerization (8-10 lectures)
    Supramolecular polymerization
         --step growth mechanism
         --systems driven by hydrogen bonding and pi-pi interaction
         --crystallization vs supramolecular polymerization
  Living supramolecular polymerization driven by hydrogen bonding
      --chain growth mechanism driven by hydrogen bonding
  Living supramolecular polymerization driven by crystallization
      --PFS block copolymer system
  Seeded supramolecular polymerization
     -- conjugated system
  Living polymerization for vesicle synthesis
     --block copolymer building blocks
3. Discussion (2 lectures) ：    
Outlook of living supramolecular polymerization
 
【授课教师简介】
Dr. Xiao-Song Wang graduated from East China University of Science and Technology in 1998 with a PhD degree in Polymer Materials and Science. After working in the University of Sussex and the University of Toronto as a postdoctoral research fellow, he received the Roberts Academic Award/RCUK Research Fellowship working at the University of Leeds in 2006. He is currently at the University of Waterloo, working in the Department of Chemistry as well as the Waterloo Institute for Nanotechnology as an Associate Professor. His research expertise spans from polymer chemistry to supramolecular functional nanomaterials. He has developed a number of novel concepts and techniques, resulting in significant breakthrough in several areas. His work on designed synthesis of block copolymer micelles opens up a field of living self-assembly; His invention of miniemulsion periphery polymerization (MEPP) offers possibility for designed synthesis of metal coordination polymer nanoparticles and functional organic and inorganic nanoshells. Recently, he invented migration insertion polymerization (MIP) and created the first example of air-stable, soluble metal-carbonyl polymers. He is currently pioneering metal-carbonyl supramolecualr chemistry.
