课程简介：
In this course we discuss several topics on the Information Optics. There are 6 sessions including Diffractive Optics, Digital Holography, Compressive Sensing, Nano-Optics, Plasmonics, Optical Tweezers and its applications to cell mechanics and drug delivery. Each session will consist of an exposure by Professor following by a discussion in class. The participants are encouraged to launch discussion on the topics of their own research interest. Depending on the interests of participants, the discussion can be on the general concept, or go to depth in the research topics. The course addresses to the community of optical signal processing, holography, diffractive and nano-optics and its applications to cell biology.
 
教学大纲：
1.    Optimization algorithm in the diffractive optical design
1.1     Sampling and oversampling;
1.2     Lohmann and Lee encoding
1.3     Gerchberg-Saxton method;
1.4     Simulated Annealing
1.5     Genetic Algorithm
1.6     Direct Binary search.
1.7      Diffractive lens
 
2     Compressive Sensing (CS)
2.1      Theory on CS; Sparsity and Restrictive Isometry Property conditions; Recovery Algorithms
2.2      Applications; Single Pixel Camera, CS Hologram; CS Imaging
 
3     Digital Holography
3.1      Digital Hologram; Phase-shifting and Frequency Shifting
3.2      Digital Holographic Microscopy
 
4     Nano-Optics: Plasmonics
4.1     Surface Plasmon Polariton
4.2     Surface Plasmon resonance sensing
4.3     Local SPP supported by nano-particles
4.4     Optical frequency plasmonic antennas
 
5     Nano-Optics: Optical trapping
5.1      Maxwell stress tensor and radiation pressure
5.2      Generalized Mie-Scattering theory, T-Matrix method
5.3      Discrete dipole approximation (DDA) algorithm
5.4      Angular momentum and torque
5.5      Finite Element Methods (FEM), Finite Difference in Time Domain (FDTD)
 
6     Cell biology and Opto-mechanics
6.1      Optical Tweezers, stretcher and time sharing tweezers
6.2      Stiffness in trapping of nanoparticles
6.3      Radiation stress and cell rheology
6.4     Targeted Drug Delivery with nanoparticles.
 
授课教师简介：
Prof. Yunlong Sheng received Baccalaureate degree from University of Science and Technology of China, Beijing in 1964, Doctorate degree in Optics in 1982, and ‘Docteur d’État’ (France) degree in Physics in 1986, both from University de Franche-Comte in France. He joints the Center for Optics, Photonics and Lasers (COPL), University Laval, Quebec Canada since 1985 and is now a full professor. His research interest involves nano-optics, optical trapping, bio cell mechanics, diffractive optics, optical signal processing, object detection and image processing. Prof. Sheng has published more than 250 technical papers and holds 2 international patents for the invention of fabrication techniques for the complex fiber Bragg gratings (FBG). Prof. Sheng has been elected as Fellow of the Optics Society of America (OSA) in 2005 and Fellow of the International Society of Optical Engineering (SPIE) in 1999. Google/scholar finds more than 4000 citations of  Prof. Sheng’s publications with the h-index of 33.
